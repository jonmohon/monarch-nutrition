## Writing a new post

1. Go to **yoursite.com/publish** and sign in.
   *(One-time: we'll set up your login together at the training session —
   after that it remembers you.)*

2. Click **Blog Posts**, then the **+** button.

3. Fill in the boxes, top to bottom:
   - **Title** — the headline of your post.
   - **Publish date** — already set to today; leave it.
   - **Description** — one or two sentences. This is what shows on the
     blog page and in Google, so make it the "why should I read this."
   - **Category** — pick the shelf it belongs on (weight management,
     kids & teens, or insurance/getting started).
   - **Cover image** — optional. If you skip it, a nice default photo for
     that category is used automatically.
   - **Post** — write here like you would in a Word doc. Highlight text
     to make it bold or a heading; the toolbar has lists and links.

4. When you're happy, click **Save** (top right), then confirm.

5. **That's it.** The site rebuilds itself — your post is live in about
   three minutes. You don't need to do anything else.

## Fixing a typo later

Same door: **yoursite.com/publish** → Blog Posts → click your post →
edit → Save. Three minutes later the fix is live.

## Deleting a post

Blog Posts → click the post → the **delete** option (trash) → confirm.

## Three things worth knowing

- **Nothing goes live until you hit Save.** Feel free to start a post and
  come back to it.
- **There's no "undo-everything" panic button needed** — every version of
  every post is kept automatically. If something ever looks wrong, tell
  us and we can restore any earlier version.
- **Your website never collects patient information**, and neither should
  a blog post — write for the public, not about specific patients.
